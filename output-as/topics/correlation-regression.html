Key Concepts

Product Moment Correlation Coefficient (PMCC)

The PMCC, denoted \(r\), measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables.

Value of \(r\)Interpretation
\(r = 1\)Perfect positive linear correlation
\(0.7 \leq r < 1\)Strong positive correlation
\(0.3 \leq r < 0.7\)Moderate positive correlation
\(0 < r < 0.3\)Weak positive correlation
\(r = 0\)No linear correlation
\(-1 < r < 0\)Negative correlation (same strength labels)
\(r = -1\)Perfect negative linear correlation

Regression Line \(y = a + bx\)

The regression line of \(y\) on \(x\) is used to predict values of \(y\) (the dependent variable) from values of \(x\) (the independent variable).

Gradient \(b\): For every 1 unit increase in \(x\), \(y\) changes by \(b\) units (on average).
Intercept \(a\): The predicted value of \(y\) when \(x = 0\) (may not always be meaningful in context).

Interpolation vs Extrapolation

Interpolation: Predicting within the range of observed data — reliable.
Extrapolation: Predicting outside the range of observed data — unreliable, because we cannot assume the linear trend continues.
Common exam mistake: When asked about limitations, always refer to the range of the original data or state that the model may not hold for extreme values.

Describing Correlation from a Regression Equation

If the gradient \(b > 0\), the correlation is positive. If \(b < 0\), the correlation is negative.

You can describe direction from the sign of \(b\), but you cannot determine strength from the equation alone (you need \(r\) for that).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Interpreting a Regression Line June 2018 Q1

A company is introducing a job evaluation scheme. Points (\(x\)) are awarded to each job based on qualifications and skills needed. Pay (\(\pounds y\)) is allocated according to the number of points. A random sample of 8 employees gave the regression equation: $$y = 4.5x - 47$$ (a) Describe the correlation between points and pay. [1]
(b) Give an interpretation of the gradient of this regression line. [1]
(c) Explain why this model might not be appropriate for all jobs. [1]
Step 1: Identify correlation direction
The gradient is \(+4.5\) (positive), so the correlation is positive.
"As the number of points increases, pay increases."
Step 2: Interpret the gradient
The gradient is 4.5, so: "For every extra point awarded, pay increases by £4.50."
Key: you must convey the idea of a rate (per point) and include the value.
Step 3: Limitations of the model
When \(x < \frac{47}{4.5} \approx 10.4\), the model predicts negative pay, which is impossible.
"For jobs with fewer than about 11 points, the model predicts negative pay, which is not realistic."
Alternatively: "The sample of 8 may not be representative of all jobs" or "We don't know the range of points used."

(a) Positive correlation

(b) For every additional point, pay increases by £4.50 (on average)

(c) For jobs with low points (below about 11), the model predicts negative pay, which is not realistic

Example 2: Stratified Sampling & Regression June 2019 Q1

A head teacher investigates the relationship between sleep (\(s\) hours) and aptitude test performance (\(p\) marks) for a sample of 40 students. The regression line of \(p\) on \(s\) is: $$p = 26.1 + 5.60s$$ (b) Describe the effect that an extra 0.5 hours of sleep may have, on average, on performance. [1]
(c) Describe one limitation of this regression model. [1]
Step 1: Effect of extra 0.5 hours
Gradient = 5.60, so each extra hour of sleep predicts 5.60 extra marks.
For 0.5 hours: \(5.60 \times 0.5 = 2.80\) extra marks.
"An extra 0.5 hours of sleep increases performance by 2.8 marks on average."
Step 2: Limitation
Possible answers:
- "The model suggests the longer you sleep the better you perform, which is unrealistic for very high values of \(s\)."
- "If \(s = 0\), the model predicts 26.1 marks regardless of other factors."
- "Only valid within the range of sleep hours observed in the data."
- "The model only considers sleep; other factors affect performance."

(b) An extra 0.5 hours of sleep is associated with an increase of 2.8 marks on average

(c) The model implies that the more you sleep, the better you perform indefinitely, which is unrealistic (e.g. sleeping 24 hours would predict a very high score)

Example 3: Negative Correlation & Extrapolation June 2022 Q1

The relationship between variables \(p\) and \(t\) is modelled by: $$p = 22 - 1.1t$$ The model is based on observations of \(t\) between 1 and 10. Where \(p\) is in cm and \(t\) is in days.
(a) Describe the correlation between \(p\) and \(t\). [1]
(b) State the units of the gradient. [1]
(c) Calculate the change in \(p\) over a 3-day period. [2]
(d) Comment on the reliability of estimating \(p\) when \(t = 19\). [1]
Step 1: Describe correlation
The gradient is \(-1.1\) (negative).
"Negative correlation" — as \(t\) increases, \(p\) decreases.
Step 2: Units of gradient
Gradient = \(\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = \frac{\text{cm}}{\text{days}}\)
Units: cm per day (cm/day)
Step 3: Change over 3 days
Change in \(p\) = gradient \(\times\) change in \(t\)
\(= -1.1 \times 3 = -3.3\) cm
\(p\) decreases by 3.3 cm over a 3-day period.
Step 4: Reliability at t = 19
The model is based on data where \(1 \leq t \leq 10\).
\(t = 19\) is outside this range (extrapolation).
"This is extrapolation, so the estimate is unreliable because the linear relationship may not hold outside the observed range of data."

(a) Negative correlation

(b) cm per day (cm/day)

(c) Decrease of 3.3 cm

(d) Unreliable because \(t = 19\) is outside the range of the data (extrapolation); the linear model may not apply beyond \(t = 10\)

Exam Tips

1. Describing correlation: State direction (positive/negative) and if given \(r\), state strength (strong/weak/moderate). Always say "linear" if appropriate.
2. Interpreting the gradient: Always include context, units, and the idea of a rate. "For every 1 [unit of x], [y] increases/decreases by [b] [units of y]."
3. Interpreting the intercept: "When \(x = 0\), the predicted value of \(y\) is \(a\)." Then comment on whether this is meaningful in context.
4. Extrapolation answers must include:
- That the value is outside the range of data
- Therefore the estimate is unreliable/may not be valid
- The relationship may not hold beyond the observed range
