Variable Acceleration

Using calculus (differentiation and integration) for non-constant acceleration problems

7/7
Sittings
7–10
Marks
Q3
Typical Position
Calculus
Method
Why this always comes up: Variable acceleration is the bridge between pure maths (calculus) and mechanics. It appears in EVERY sitting because it tests differentiation, integration, and physical interpretation all in one question. It is usually the last mechanics question and worth 8-10 marks.

Key Concepts

The calculus relationships

If displacement = \(s(t)\), velocity = \(v(t)\), acceleration = \(a(t)\):

  • \(v = \dfrac{ds}{dt}\) — differentiate displacement to get velocity
  • \(a = \dfrac{dv}{dt} = \dfrac{d^2s}{dt^2}\) — differentiate velocity to get acceleration
  • \(s = \displaystyle\int v \, dt\) — integrate velocity to get displacement
  • \(v = \displaystyle\int a \, dt\) — integrate acceleration to get velocity

Key problem types at AS Level

  • "At rest": Set \(v = 0\) and solve for \(t\)
  • "Instantaneous rest": Same as above — particle momentarily stops
  • "Stops accelerating": Set \(a = 0\) and solve for \(t\)
  • "Find distance": Integrate \(v\) with limits, watch for direction changes
  • "Total distance": Must split at \(v = 0\) points and add magnitudes
  • "Changes direction": Happens when \(v\) changes sign (passes through zero)
"Solutions relying on calculator technology are not acceptable" means you MUST show the algebraic differentiation/integration steps. This instruction appears on almost every variable acceleration question.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Find acceleration from \(v(t)\) 4 marks

Based on: June 2020 Q3 | View Paper

Question: A particle P moves along a straight line such that at time \(t\) seconds, \(t \geq 0\), after leaving O, the velocity of P is \(v = (7 - 2t)(t + 2)\) m s\(^{-1}\). Find the value of \(t\) at the instant when P stops accelerating.

Step 1: Expand the velocity expression M1

\(v = (7 - 2t)(t + 2) = 7t + 14 - 2t^2 - 4t = -2t^2 + 3t + 14\)

Step 2: Differentiate to find acceleration M1 A1

\(a = \dfrac{dv}{dt} = -4t + 3\)

Step 3: Set \(a = 0\) and solve A1

"Stops accelerating" means \(a = 0\):

\(-4t + 3 = 0\)

\(t = \dfrac{3}{4}\) seconds

Example 2: Find distance from \(v(t)\) 5 marks

Based on: June 2020 Q3 | View Paper

Question: With \(v = (7 - 2t)(t + 2)\), find the distance of P from O at the instant when P changes its direction of motion.

Step 1: Find when direction changes (\(v = 0\)) M1

\((7 - 2t)(t + 2) = 0\)

\(t = 3.5\) or \(t = -2\)

Since \(t \geq 0\), direction changes at \(t = 3.5\)

Step 2: Integrate velocity to find displacement M1 A1

\(s = \displaystyle\int_0^{3.5} (-2t^2 + 3t + 14) \, dt\)

\(= \left[-\dfrac{2t^3}{3} + \dfrac{3t^2}{2} + 14t\right]_0^{3.5}\)

Step 3: Evaluate A1 A1

\(= -\dfrac{2(3.5)^3}{3} + \dfrac{3(3.5)^2}{2} + 14(3.5)\)

\(= -\dfrac{2(42.875)}{3} + \dfrac{3(12.25)}{2} + 49\)

\(= -28.583... + 18.375 + 49\)

\(= 38.79...\) m

\(= \dfrac{1029}{27} = \dfrac{343}{9} = 38\frac{1}{9}\) m

Keep fractions exact where possible. The mark scheme often gives the answer as both a fraction and a decimal. If the question says "3 s.f." then round at the end only.

Example 3: Total distance with direction change 8 marks

Based on: June 2023 Q3 | View Paper

Question: A particle P moves along a straight line such that at time \(t\) seconds, \(t \geq 0\), after passing through O, the velocity of P is \(v = 15 - t^2 - 2t\) m s\(^{-1}\). Verify that P comes to rest when \(t = 3\). Find the total distance travelled by P in the interval \(0 \leq t \leq 4\).

Step 1: Verify \(v = 0\) when \(t = 3\) B1

\(v(3) = 15 - 9 - 6 = 0\) ✓

P is at rest when \(t = 3\)

Step 2: Recognise direction change at \(t = 3\) M1

For \(t < 3\): \(v > 0\) (moving in positive direction)

For \(t > 3\): \(v < 0\) (moving in negative direction)

So total distance = |displacement from 0 to 3| + |displacement from 3 to 4|

Step 3: Integrate from 0 to 3 M1 A1

\(\displaystyle\int_0^3 (15 - t^2 - 2t) \, dt = \left[15t - \frac{t^3}{3} - t^2\right]_0^3\)

\(= 45 - 9 - 9 = 27\) m

Step 4: Integrate from 3 to 4 M1 A1

\(\displaystyle\int_3^4 (15 - t^2 - 2t) \, dt = \left[15t - \frac{t^3}{3} - t^2\right]_3^4\)

\(= \left(60 - \frac{64}{3} - 16\right) - 27\)

\(= 44 - \frac{64}{3} - 27 = 17 - \frac{64}{3} = \frac{51 - 64}{3} = -\frac{13}{3}\)

Step 5: Total distance = sum of magnitudes A1

Total distance \(= 27 + \left|-\dfrac{13}{3}\right| = 27 + \dfrac{13}{3} = \dfrac{81 + 13}{3} = \dfrac{94}{3}\) m

\(= 31\frac{1}{3}\) m (or 31.3 m to 3 s.f.)

The most common error: students integrate from 0 to 4 in one go and get 27 - 13/3 = 68/3. This gives NET displacement, not TOTAL DISTANCE. You must split at the point where v = 0 and take magnitudes.

Example 4: Displacement given, find acceleration at rest 9 marks

Based on: June 2022 Q3 | View Paper

Question: A particle P moves along a straight line. At time \(t\) seconds, \(t \geq 0\), the distance of P from O is given by \(s = \frac{1}{3}t^3 - \frac{5}{2}t^2 + 6t\). Find the acceleration of P at each of the times when P is at instantaneous rest.

Step 1: Differentiate \(s\) to find \(v\) M1 A1

\(v = \dfrac{ds}{dt} = t^2 - 5t + 6\)

Step 2: Find when at rest (\(v = 0\)) M1 A1

\(t^2 - 5t + 6 = 0\)

\((t - 2)(t - 3) = 0\)

\(t = 2\) or \(t = 3\)

Step 3: Differentiate \(v\) to find \(a\) M1

\(a = \dfrac{dv}{dt} = 2t - 5\)

Step 4: Evaluate acceleration at each time A1

When \(t = 2\): \(a = 2(2) - 5 = -1\) m s\(^{-2}\)

When \(t = 3\): \(a = 2(3) - 5 = 1\) m s\(^{-2}\)

Common Pitfalls

  • Distance vs displacement: \(\int v \, dt\) gives displacement. For distance, split at \(v = 0\) points.
  • Forgetting +C: If no limits given, include constant of integration and use initial conditions.
  • Not expanding before differentiating: Expand products like \((7-2t)(t+2)\) before differentiating term by term.
  • Sign of acceleration: Negative acceleration does NOT always mean decelerating — it means accelerating in the negative direction.
  • "Magnitude of acceleration": Take the absolute value of \(a\) when asked for magnitude.
