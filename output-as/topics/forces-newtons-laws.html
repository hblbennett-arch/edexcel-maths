Forces & Newton's Laws

F = ma applied to towing problems, lifts, and driving force minus resistance

3/7
Sittings
5–12
Marks
Q4
Typical Position
F = ma
Core Equation
Why this matters: Forces questions test the fundamental principle of mechanics — Newton's second law. At AS, these appear as towing problems (car + trailer) or lift problems (apparent weight). They require careful identification of all forces on each object and correct application of F = ma. When the towbar breaks or conditions change, a follow-up tests your ability to adapt.

Key Concepts

Newton's Laws at AS Level

  • N1: If net force = 0, object stays at rest or moves at constant velocity
  • N2: \(F_{\text{net}} = ma\) (resultant force = mass × acceleration)
  • N3: Action and reaction are equal and opposite (used for tension in towbar)

Towing problems — the method

  1. Whole system: Apply F = ma to entire system to find acceleration or resistance
    \(\text{Driving force} - \text{Total resistance} = (m_{\text{car}} + m_{\text{trailer}}) \times a\)
  2. Individual object: Apply F = ma to trailer alone (or car alone) to find tension \(T\)
    Trailer: \(T - R_{\text{trailer}} = m_{\text{trailer}} \times a\)
  3. After towbar breaks: Apply F = ma to each separately with T = 0

Lift problems — the method

  • Accelerating up: Normal reaction > Weight → \(R - mg = ma\) → \(R = m(g + a)\)
  • Accelerating down: Weight > Normal reaction → \(mg - R = ma\) → \(R = m(g - a)\)
  • Constant speed or stationary: \(R = mg\)
  • The "apparent weight" felt by a person = the normal reaction force
In towing problems, you can always check your answer: the tension must be less than the driving force (otherwise the car itself would decelerate). If your tension comes out negative, check your sign convention.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Lift with block — find tension in rope and force on block 6 marks

Based on: June 2022 Q4 | View Paper

Question: A lift cage (mass 40 kg) carries a block (mass 10 kg). The lift is raised by a vertical rope with constant acceleration 0.2 m s\(^{-2}\). Find (a) the tension in the rope PQ, (b) the magnitude of the force exerted on the block by the lift cage.

Step 1: F = ma for the whole system (lift + block) M1 A1

Total mass = 40 + 10 = 50 kg

Taking up as positive:

\(T - 50g = 50 \times 0.2\)

\(T = 50(9.8) + 10 = 490 + 10 = 500\) N

Step 2: Verify — tension in rope PQ A1

Tension in rope = 500 N

Step 3: F = ma for the block alone M1 A1

Forces on block: Normal reaction \(R\) upward (from cage floor), weight \(10g\) downward.

Taking up as positive:

\(R - 10g = 10 \times 0.2\)

\(R = 10(9.8) + 2 = 98 + 2 = 100\) N

Step 4: State answer A1

Force on block by lift cage = 100 N (upwards)

Check: This is greater than the block's weight (98 N) — makes sense because the lift is accelerating upwards.

For lift problems: if accelerating up, every object inside "feels heavier" (R > mg). If accelerating down, everything "feels lighter" (R < mg). At constant speed, R = mg exactly. This is why you feel heavy when a lift starts going up.

Example 2: Car towing trailer — find acceleration and tension 7 marks

Based on: June 2023 Q4 | View Paper

Question: A car (1200 kg) tows a trailer (400 kg) on a horizontal road. Car resistance = \(2R\) N, trailer resistance = \(R\) N. Driving force = 7400 N, tension = 2400 N. Find the value of \(a\) (acceleration).

Step 1: F = ma for the trailer M1 A1

Forces on trailer (horizontal): Tension \(T = 2400\) N forward, resistance \(R\) backward.

\(2400 - R = 400a\) ... (1)

Step 2: F = ma for the whole system M1 A1

Whole system (car + trailer), total mass = 1600 kg:

\(7400 - 2R - R = 1600a\)

\(7400 - 3R = 1600a\) ... (2)

Step 3: Solve simultaneously M1 A1

From (1): \(R = 2400 - 400a\)

Substitute into (2): \(7400 - 3(2400 - 400a) = 1600a\)

\(7400 - 7200 + 1200a = 1600a\)

\(200 = 400a\)

\(a = 0.5\) m s\(^{-2}\) A1

(And \(R = 2400 - 200 = 2200\) N)

Example 3: Towbar breaks — trailer decelerates 12 marks total

Based on: June 2024 Q4 | View Paper

Question: A car (800 kg) tows a trailer (600 kg). Car resistance = 400 N, trailer resistance = \(R\) N. Driving force = 1740 N, acceleration = 0.6 m s\(^{-2}\). (a) Show \(R = 500\). (b) Find tension \(T\). The towbar breaks at 12.5 m s\(^{-1}\). (c) Show deceleration = \(\frac{5}{6}\) m s\(^{-2}\). (d) Find distance \(d\) before trailer stops.

Step 1: Whole system F = ma to find R M1 A1 A1

Total mass = 800 + 600 = 1400 kg

\(1740 - 400 - R = 1400 \times 0.6\)

\(1340 - R = 840\)

\(R = 500\) N as required

Step 2: Trailer alone to find T M1 A1 A1

For trailer: \(T - R = 600 \times 0.6\)

\(T - 500 = 360\)

\(T = 860\) N

Step 3: After towbar breaks — deceleration of trailer B1

Towbar breaks → T = 0. Only force on trailer = resistance = 500 N (backward).

\(F = ma\): \(-500 = 600 \times a\)

\(a = -\dfrac{500}{600} = -\dfrac{5}{6}\) m s\(^{-2}\)

Deceleration = \(\dfrac{5}{6}\) m s\(^{-2}\) as required.

Step 4: Distance before trailer stops M1 A1 A1

For trailer after break: \(u = 12.5\), \(v = 0\), \(a = -\frac{5}{6}\)

Using \(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\):

\(0 = 12.5^2 + 2\left(-\dfrac{5}{6}\right)d\)

\(\dfrac{10d}{6} = 156.25\)

\(d = \dfrac{156.25 \times 6}{10} = \dfrac{937.5}{10} = 93.75\) m

Or: \(d = 93.8\) m (3 s.f.)

The "towbar breaks" scenario is the most common follow-up in towing questions. Remember: after the break, each object moves independently. The car may accelerate (driving force still acts but now only moves the car), while the trailer decelerates (only resistance acts). Use the speed at the moment of breaking as the initial speed for both.

Common Pitfalls

  • Confusing tension and driving force: The driving force acts on the CAR only, not on the trailer. The tension in the towbar is what pulls the trailer.
  • Applying F = ma to wrong object: Clearly state "For the trailer:" or "For the whole system:" before each equation.
  • Missing resistance on the car: The car has BOTH a driving force forward AND resistance backward, PLUS the tension pulling backward (via Newton's third law, the trailer pulls back on the car).
  • Lift problems — wrong direction for a: Deceleration going up is equivalent to acceleration going down. Think about which direction the NET force points.
  • After towbar breaks — using wrong mass: Now each object has its own mass in F = ma.
